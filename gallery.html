Pet Care Tips & FAQs

What age should puppies be before adoption?

Puppies should be at least 8 weeks old before leaving their mother. This allows proper socialization with littermates and ensures they've received essential early-life nutrition. Many breeders recommend waiting until 10-12 weeks for optimal development.

How do I prepare my home for a new kitten?

Create a safe space with litter box, food/water bowls, scratching post, and toys. Remove toxic plants, secure loose wires, and block small spaces where kittens could get stuck. Set up a quiet room for initial adjustment before introducing them to the full house.

What vaccinations do puppies need?

Puppies typically need DHPP (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza) at 6-8 weeks, 10-12 weeks, and 14-16 weeks. Rabies vaccine is given at 12-16 weeks. Your veterinarian will provide a complete vaccination schedule based on your puppy's needs.

How much does it cost to care for a pet?

Annual costs for puppies range from $1,500-$2,000 including food, veterinary care, supplies, and training. Kittens cost $800-$1,500 annually. Initial setup costs (crate, bed, supplies) add $200-$500. Emergency vet visits and unexpected expenses should also be budgeted.

What should I feed my new puppy or kitten?

Feed high-quality puppy or kitten formula food appropriate for their age. Puppies need 3-4 small meals daily until 6 months, then 2 meals daily. Kittens need 4 meals daily until 6 months, then 2-3 meals. Always provide fresh water and consult your vet for specific recommendations.
